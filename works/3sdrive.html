Landing Page

A landing page aim to promote the Android app, Driving Assistance.

·   ·   ·

Overview

After the supervisor from a startup seeing my resume and portfolio at job sites, he decided to corporate this landing page with me as a freelance. This landing page aims to promote its Android app, Omnie, a personal driving voice assistance that offers a smarter and safer way to use phones while driving. Users can get the navigation way, play music, call friends or even send messages by asking it questions or telling it to do things while driving. It features that it is safe, simple and easy to use.

Company: 3sDrive (startup)

Role: UX/UI Design, Responsive Web Design

Time: 2018/9-2019/2 (part-time, freelance)

·   ·   ·

Think Before Design

Reviewing the original design is for not to repeat the mistake again. I have three main suggestions for the original design.

Problem #1 - The picture is very FAR AWAY from their brand spirit
The picture of the origin front-page design will let people notice that ice, snow and heavy-duty vehicles, give me the feeling of being strong, heavy and cold. Maybe they want to highlight their durable, however, It is very far away from the brand spirit, safe, simple and easy to use.

The original design is very far away from their brand spirit.

Problem #2 - “read more” should not be the most obvious button
In addition, the most obvious button, Call to Action(CTAs) button, in the front page is "read more", I can feel the desire to let users continue reading it, but I think that every company does this landing page is not merely to introduce the product. Their purpose of a landing page is converting which allows potential customers who enter this page to convert into real purchases, downloads, community tracking, and more. Therefore, I think the most important button is to "download". Moreover, I recommend the CTAs should be descriptive, so it should be "download it now!" or "try it now!".

Problem #3 - The conflicting color scheme doesn’t fit its brand spirit
Since the brand spirit is safe, simple and easy to use, our purpose of this website is to make users feel relaxed and simple. However, the original color scheme is green and orange-red which let me feel so conflicting. Therefore, when it comes to color choices, I tend to choose colors that are harmonious rather than conflicting.

The color of original design is conflicting.
·   ·   ·

Design Thinking

Solution #1 - First image of the landing page
To solve these problems, we need to focus on the product, personal driving voice assistance and its feature which is safe, simple and easy to use. Therefore, I use a straight road with grass around as the background to imply that it is very safe, simple and relax. Moreover, I use their advertisement slogan into a dialog way to imply that it a voice-related product.

Solution #2 - The arrangement of CTA buttons
I proposed that there are three Call-to-Actions(CTA) buttons for the landing page:
1. Download the Android app
2. Follow the company’s social media
3. Read the whole page

According to the three CTAs, I redesign the front page. I use a purple gradient color that helps the first CTA button, “Download it now”, which is the most important button in this page to stand out. I also put the social media link at the top of the navbar but just in a text form not in a distinct color or background. Finally, I put a down icon as read more to hint users to read the whole page.

Solution #3 - Color Scheme
In order not to be so conflicting, I give up the original color scheme, green and orange-red. I use a green-blue color (green stands for safe, easy to use and blue stands for simple, professional) to be the primary color and a gradient color from pink to purple as an accent color.
