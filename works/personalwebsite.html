Overview

Every time when I use my personal website as my portfolio for my job applications, the interviewers may comment some feedback about it, such as “I don't know which work is the most important”, “Works should be the most important part of your website”, or “Your category is not clear”. Therefore, it let me embark on a redesign journey.

This website serves as a part of my job applications, and interviewers are the users. I collected all the advice and found out the problems to have a new arrangement for my website. I appreciate every feedback I got which become my stepping stone in improving my website for the better.

Role: UX/UI Design, Responsive Web Design

Time: 2017/1 - NOW

Github: yuyuchinglin.github.io (Github Page)

·   ·   ·

Qualitative Interviews

When I went to a job interview, I will ask them their feelings and suggestions about my portfolio website in the 1-1 section. I interviewed people of age group between 28-50 years, who was a supervisor with technology, design or management backgrounds.

“You did a great job. I can see you take it very seriously. However, there are some suggestions. When I visited to the website, I want to see your portfolio, the portfolio should be the most important part.”
- Blockchain Tech Lead, Male, 42 years old

“There are almost ten works on your website. Some of them are design work, and some of them are coding work. The works’ category should be more clear. Besides, I don’t know which is the most important work. You should highlight your favorite work.”
- Project Manager, Female, 35 years old

“The color scheme on your website doesn’t look nice. I felt a little dirty because your background is not black enough. I think the color should be more clear.”
- Software Engineer, 29 years old

·   ·   ·

Problems & Solutions

According to the interview, there are two main problems. One is works, the other is the color scheme. My solutions are provided below.

(1) Works:
Instead of putting a lot of introduction, skills, pictures on my index page, now I only put my works on my index page to highlight the importance of portfolio. I reduce the number of works to ensure portfolio quality. Furthermore, I put a filter on my works. If users want to see coding work, they can use the filter and it will show all of the coding work.

(2) Color:
In the second version, I want to be professional, elegant and warm, so I change my color scheme. To reduce interviewers’ cognitive loading, I use a white background, which is the same as most portfolio websites, instead of a black background.
