IoT Device Management

Users can remotely organize, update, and monitor their devices at scale.

·   ·   ·

Overview

Our company produces and sells lots of tablet computers for military and industrial purposes. In the past, if we want to update the firmware or software, we can only plug into the computer. It’s not very convenient because we have a lot of tablets. Therefore, we create a cloud service to manage and control our devices.

Role: UX/UI, Front-end Designer

Time: 2019/3 - Recent (full-time)

·   ·   ·

Build My Workflow

This is the suitable workflow that I have developed for the enterprise product (B2B) in the limited time and resource. I will introduce every step in the following.

My workflow
·   ·   ·

Step1. Mind Map

Our product is an enterprise product focuses on IoT device management which can be a really difficult concept for general users or me to understand. Initially, I intend to communicate with individuals who are very familiar with the product and utilize a mind map to represent people’s thoughts about the operational process.

Mind map of our website
·   ·   ·

Step2. Low-fidelity Prototype (Wireframe)

The next step will be to transfer the complex model into interactive design. Since our website contains many functions, enacting wireframes to focus on user flow is necessary. Additionally, utilizing a low-fidelity prototype approach will allow us to test ideas before we expend expensive design and developmental resources.

Low-fidelity prototype (wireframe) of our website
·   ·   ·

Step3. Discussion & Usability Testing

We will invite certain people to conduct usability testing. We will ask him/her to execute a specific action while expressing his/her thoughts and feelings throughout the process. For example, I may ask him/her to activate the KIOSK mode of devices whose serial numbers are “SN-ANDROID-12,” “SN-ANDROID-13,” and “SN-ANDROID-14.” However, not all feedback will identify the underlying cause of user frustration. It will be important for me to critically analyze the design issues that are negatively affecting users’ feelings towards the design.

While doing visual design, I focus more on the consistency. In this time, I should create a UI system to be a reference for development and provide a high-fidelity product prototype to be an ideal image of the product.

Project Learnings

Design and development is iterative
Iterations are very valuable when it comes to learning from past versions, seeing the possibility of the good that can come out of it. Although I did a lot of preparation for the design and proposed the best possible version, many problems still occurred during and/or after developing the whole website. I changed my prototype many times, and I believe that it is best to think about the big picture while redesigning it. Therefore, I learned that only iterative design can come close to being perfect.

Having ideas or opinions before meetings
Our company holds many meetings. I discovered that if people did not have ideas or opinions beforehand, they will be easily influenced by others’ opinions. This makes it difficult to reach a good conclusion during our discussions, since we do not have enough time or clear thoughts to draw such a conclusion. Therefore, I believe that having ideas or opinions before group discussions is best in order to promote deep and high-quality discussions and conclusions.
